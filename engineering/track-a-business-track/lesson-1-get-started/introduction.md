# Introduction

If you've ever been in a meeting where someone said, "the deployment broke and nobody knows why," this course was built for you.

You don't need to be technical to take it. You don't need to know what Git is, what a sandbox is, or what "metadata" means. If you work in Salesforce, as an admin, a release manager, a project lead, a RevOps specialist, or as someone who signs the check for a development team, this course will give you a clear-eyed view of why Salesforce releases fail, and what kind of solution actually fixes the problem.

## Key takeaways

By the time you finish this course, you'll be able to:

* Explain why Salesforce DevOps is different from standard software development practices, and why that difference matters.
* Name the seven specific ways standard tools fail against Salesforce's architecture, and what each one costs in real terms.
* Make the case for a purpose-built approach to a CFO, a VP of Engineering, or a skeptical colleague.

You will not be a Salesforce developer when you finish. You won't be configuring pipelines or writing deployment scripts. The goal is understanding, the kind that lets you ask the right questions, evaluate the right solutions, and explain the problem to anyone in the room.

## How to follow this course

This course is organized into six Lessons. Each one builds on the last. You can read at your own pace, without needing a Salesforce login. Plan for four to five hours total, spread across however many sessions work for you.

## Where this fits in the curriculum

This is the foundation lesson of the Flosum certification curriculum, the starting point for everyone, regardless of which subsequent courses you pursue. Think of it as the shared language. Before you can understand how a solution works, you need to understand exactly what it's solving.
